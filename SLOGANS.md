# Qwickword — Slogan & Subtitle Candidates

## Core positioning (candidates for the permanent subtitle under the wordmark)
- [ ] Set a time. Have the word. Hang up.
- [ ] The meeting that isn't allowed to run long.
- [ ] Talk fast, hang up on time.
- [ ] A meeting with a seatbelt.
- [ ] Every Qwickword ends. That's the whole idea.
- [ ] Short by design, not by accident.
- [ ] No extend button. Anywhere. Ever.

## Meeting culture, generally
- [ ] This meeting could have been a Qwickword.
- [ ] No agenda, no overtime, no excuse.
- [ ] The opposite of "let's take this offline."
- [ ] For when "quick sync" turns into an hour.
- [ ] "Just five more minutes" has never once been five minutes.
- [ ] Built for people who've heard "let's circle back" one too many times.
- [ ] Nobody has ever left a Qwickword wondering where the last 45 minutes went.
- [ ] Somewhere right now a meeting that should've ended an hour ago is still going. Not this one.
- [ ] The best meetings are the ones you forget by lunch, because they ended on time.
- [ ] A calendar invite that keeps its promise.

## Developers & creators (standups, sprints, "this could've been a Slack message")
- [ ] Standup, but it actually stands up in two minutes.
- [ ] Shorter than your last sprint retro.
- [ ] Fewer meetings, more commits.
- [ ] The standup that doesn't turn into a design review.
- [ ] Built by people who've sat through a "quick sync" that wasn't quick.
- [ ] So the deploy doesn't wait on the meeting.
- [ ] For people who'd rather be shipping.
- [ ] Your calendar already has enough recurring events.
- [ ] Ten minutes, tops, because the PR isn't going to review itself.

## For the person running the team
- [ ] Know your team's back at their desks in five.
- [ ] Trust, but time-box.
- [ ] The meeting you can actually fit between two other meetings.
- [ ] Give people their afternoon back.
- [ ] Run a tight one on purpose.
- [ ] Prove the meeting was worth it by how fast it ended.
- [ ] If it can't be said in three minutes, it probably wasn't urgent.

## Short and blunt
- [ ] Say it. Send it. Done.
- [ ] Time-boxed, by design.
- [ ] It ends. That's the feature.
- [ ] Quick, on purpose.
- [ ] Meetings, minus the bloat.

## Longer lines (good for a rotating footer, more room to land the joke)
- [ ] Every other tool brags about no time limit. We brag about the opposite.
- [ ] The call ends at zero, not whenever everyone finally stops talking.
- [ ] You'd be surprised how many good ideas fit in three minutes, once you're forced to find out.
- [ ] Most meetings don't need an hour. Most meetings don't even need thirty minutes. Try five.
- [ ] It's not a video call app. It's a video call with an ending, which is apparently rare enough to sell.
